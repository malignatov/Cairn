# great_life_dimensions

The user's PERMA-V intake: importance and satisfaction ratings across
the six dimensions of a flourishing life from Seligman's positive
psychology. This table captures *definition*, not action — it says what
the user wants their life to be aimed at, in their own words.

## Fields

- `id` — uuid, generated by the server.
- `dimension` *(required)* — one of:
    - `positive_emotions` — pleasure, joy, contentment in daily life
    - `engagement` — flow, absorption, being deeply involved
    - `relationships` — close, meaningful connections with others
    - `meaning` — connection to something larger; a sense of purpose
    - `achievement` — mastery, progress, visible accomplishment
    - `vitality` — health, energy, physical wellbeing
- `assessed_at` — server-set.
- `importance` *(required)* — integer 0–10. How central is this to the
  user's idea of a great life?
- `satisfaction` *(required)* — integer 0–10. How is it going right now?
- `notes` — optional free text; one sentence per dimension is the norm.
- `session_id` — groups all six dimensions from one intake.

## Notes

- A complete session has six rows (or fewer if the user genuinely doesn't
  resonate with a dimension). The skill captures whatever the user
  produces — don't pressure them to rate something they can't.
- This table is the orientation signal for the scanner. A dimension with
  high importance and low satisfaction is a flag; an SLU touching that
  dimension carries extra weight in blindspot detection.
- Re-run yearly, or after major life events. Definition evolves; the
  system should know when it does.
