# resources

Finite quantities the user has and tracks at a **strategic** level —
cash runway, monthly burn, deep-work hours per week. Not a budgeting
tool. Resources hold current values, not histories. If a feature
would belong in Mint or YNAB, it doesn't belong here.

## Fields

- `id` — uuid, generated by the server.
- `name` *(required)* — what's being tracked. *"Cash runway (months)"*,
  *"Monthly burn (EUR)"*, *"Deep work hours per week"*.
- `category` *(required)* — one of `money` | `time` | `energy` | `other`.
- `unit` *(required)* — *"EUR"*, *"USD"*, *"months"*, *"hours_per_week"*,
  *"count"*, etc. Free text; pick something that means something to
  you and stay consistent.
- `current_quantity` *(required)* — the number, as of `as_of`.
- `as_of` *(required)* — when `current_quantity` was last truthful.
  Must be updated together with `current_quantity` — the storage
  layer enforces that.
- `floor` — minimum acceptable level. Below this is a strong signal
  to the scanner. Null when you don't want a floor.
- `burn_rate` — units per month flowing out. Only meaningful for
  depleting resources (cash, time blocks). Null when not applicable.
- `replenish_rate` — units per month flowing in. Net depletion is
  `burn_rate - replenish_rate`; runway computes from net depletion.
- `notes` — free-form.
- `created_at`, `updated_at` — server-managed.

## No status field

Resources don't have lifecycle states. A resource is either tracked
(it's in the table) or not (delete it). Use `state_delete` to retire
one. This is the only entity type in Cairn for which `state_delete`
is allowed — everything else has a status transition instead.

## Notes

- Update via `skill://update_resources`, not by hand. The skill walks
  every row and asks for the current number, keeping `as_of` honest.
- The scanner treats `as_of` older than 60 days as stale and refuses
  to draw conclusions about runway from out-of-date numbers. Refresh
  regularly enough that the scanner can actually do its job.
- Cairn does not store individual transactions. If you want to track
  spending categorically, use a budgeting app and bring the strategic
  signal (runway, monthly burn) over.
- Money-related observations from the scanner are framed gently and
  never prescriptive. Cairn is not a financial advisor; it surfaces
  numbers and lets you interpret.
