# life_units

The Strack Strategic Life Units. Sixteen categories spanning the surface
of an adult life — broad enough that nearly any activity tags to at
least one, narrow enough that a tag carries real information.

On first boot, the table is seeded with all 16 defaults. The user can
deactivate units that don't apply (no significant other right now, say)
and re-activate them later. Custom units are allowed; they're the
exception, not the norm.

## Fields

- `id` — uuid, generated by the server.
- `name` *(required)* — short, recognizable label.
- `area` *(required)* — Strategic Life Area; one of:
    - `relationships`
    - `body_mind_spirit`
    - `community_society`
    - `job_learning_finances`
    - `interests_entertainment`
    - `personal_care`
- `description` — one or two sentences. Used by the LLM to decide which
  unit a captured entity touches.
- `active` — boolean (default `true`). Inactive units stay in the table
  for trajectory history but are skipped by `list_active_slus` and by
  the portfolio assessment.
- `custom` — boolean (default `false`). The 16 Strack defaults are
  `custom = false`; anything the user adds is `custom = true`.
- `created_at`, `updated_at` — server-managed.

## The 16 defaults

  Relationships:           Significant other, Family, Friendship
  Body, Mind, Spirit:      Physical health, Mental health, Spirituality
  Community and Society:   Community involvement, Societal engagement
  Job, Learning, Finances: Job and career, Education and learning, Finances
  Interests, Entertainment:Hobbies and interests, Online entertainment,
                           Offline entertainment
  Personal Care:           Physiological needs, Activities of daily living

## Notes

- Don't rename the 16 defaults. Trajectory data is keyed to them; renames
  silently break long-running blindspot detection.
- Deactivation is reversible. Hide, don't delete.
