# decisions

A decision is a choice the user made with a stated rationale. The point of
recording one is so a future version of the user can argue with their past
self — what looked obvious then, what they'd considered, and whether the
prediction held.

## Fields

- `id` — uuid, generated by the server.
- `project_id` — optional foreign key into `projects`. Many decisions are
  cross-cutting; leaving this null is fine.
- `what` *(required)* — the decision itself, in plain language. One or two
  sentences.
- `rationale` *(required)* — why this and not the alternatives. If the user
  can't articulate a rationale, don't record the decision — ask.
- `alternatives_considered` — what else was on the table. This is the
  field that ages best; capture it generously.
- `decided_at` — server default: now.
- `revisit_at` — when (if ever) this should come back for review. Null means
  "stand by it until something changes."
- `outcome` — filled in at revisit time. Was the call right? What surprised
  you? Don't write the outcome at decision time — that's just confidence.

## Notes

- Decisions are immutable in spirit. Update `outcome` and `revisit_at`, but
  resist editing `what` or `rationale` — a wrong decision honestly recorded
  is more valuable than a tidy one rewritten in hindsight.
- If a decision is reversed, record the reversal as a new decision that
  references the prior one in its `rationale`.
